# Week 5 Day 4 · English Speaking Practice

## Topic

Separating mastery evidence from presentation artifacts.

## Speaking Script

Today I clarified an important boundary in my learning project: evidence of understanding is different from a polished presentation. My own code, raw experiment results, spoken explanations, and delayed rebuilds are the evidence. A dashboard, demo, or speaking script is only a presentation artifact built from that evidence.

This distinction matters because a polished demo can accidentally hide a gap in understanding, especially when AI helps with wording or visualization. To prevent that, I now use a source gate. Every confirmed claim must point back to my notes, measurements, or rebuild record. Anything still uncertain stays labeled as an open question instead of becoming a confident conclusion.

The sequence is simple: first prove the idea, then polish it. Even the review mode in the frontend is only a content view. It does not mean the topic has passed the learning check.
